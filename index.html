Win a trip to Europe

itinerary

Day 1

 Night-time transfer to the Montmartre neighbourhood | Funicular ride (included). Welcome to Paris, the city of love and romance. The capital of France is a global centre for art and fashion and is a traveller’s paradise. Upon your arrival at the airport, you would be transferred to the hotel. A guide would be ready at the hotel to give an overview of the tour. At the end of the evening, it would be time to go to the Montmartre district. This neighbourhood district is known for its painters, artists, and its cabarets. Despite the number tourists, it remains the most unabashedly romantic part of Paris. Enjoy a magical night alongside your dinner. You can choose between Indian, Chinese, French and American cuisines. Later, ascend by funicular to the hill and admire the panoramic view of Paris from the top. Return to the hotel for an overnight stay.
   

Day 2

 City tour in Paris. Boat ride along the Seine. Climb to the second floor of the Eiffel Tower. Evening transfer. Embark on a scenic guided tour to discover the breath-taking monuments, main boulevards and the history of Paris. You would have the opportunity to climb up to the second floor of the remarkable metal monument, Eiffel Tower. Built in 1889, it is one of the most visited monuments in Paris. End your visit with a boat cruise along the River Seine, which divides the north of Paris from the south. You have the afternoon at leisure to explore the area on your own pace. At sunset, enjoy dinner in Quartier Latin, a very picturesque area of the historic centre where a wide variety of international cuisines await you. Return to hotel for an overnight stay.
   

Day 3

 Free time in the City of Light. Explore the centre of Paris by arriving at the Louvre Museum. A world of art is waiting to be discovered as it includes variety of French and European painting, objects and sculptures from the Middle Age. Later, you would have free time to explore the city on your own. You can explore Benlux, the international centre of fashion accessories and luxury items in Paris. Later, return to the hotel for a comfortable overnight stay.
   

Day 4

 Vineyards in Beaune and Bourgogne region. Today, you would travel to the centre of France to the Bourgogne region, which is famous for its Burgundy wines. The area is studded with grand chateaux and criss-crossed by a network of canals. You would make a stop in Noyers, a small mediaeval town classified amongst the prettiest places in France. After this, you would visit Beaune, in the centre of the Bourgogne region. Its streets are full of flowers; its hospice and wine museum will be a memorable part of the trip. Enjoy a delicious lunch before heading to Lyon. You would arrive at Lyon, the capital of Roman Gallia on the banks of the Rhône, in mid-afternoon. Its historic centre has been declared a World Heritage site. You have rest of the evening at leisure, so you can explore the place on your own. At the end of the day, retire to your hotel for a comfortable overnight stay.
   

Day 5

 Swiss Landscapes. Centre of Geneva Today, you would travel to Switzerland which is a mountainous country with numerous lakes flowing through it. Upon your arrival in Geneva, you would be headed to the places with a guide. You would first head to the Palais des Nations (the United Nations headquarters in Europe) and then to the beautiful Jardin Anglais. Built in 1855, it is an urban park in Geneva made to visually improve the wooden harbour. Admire the charming monumental fountain and the flower clock in the park. Then, you would get a chance to admire the Jet d’Eau, the tallest fountain in Geneva. After lunch, the tour will continue through Switzerland passing by the beautiful Leman and Neuchatel Lakes (note: depending on the number of travellers from Geneva to Zürich, this trip might be done by train). Leman is shaped like a crescent and overlooked by the Alps. The lake shares border with Franc and Switzerland. Later, retire to the hotel for a comfortable overnight stay.
   

Day 6

 Boat trip across Zurich Lake | Journey by train. With a guide, stroll in the most populated city of Switzerland, the financial and cultural capital of the country. Board a boat for a ride in the Zurich Lake and enjoy the scenic view for almost 2 hours. The boat will make brief stops for you to admire the villages on the way. You would get down in Rapperswil, a tiny but picturesque town of roses overlooked by its medieval castle, perched high on the hill. Then, you would return to Zurich by train, and witness the efficiency and punctuality of the Swiss Railways. Enjoy the evening in the historic centre, next to the lake, where you can dine at your favourite restaurant (Indian, Oriental, Italian, International, Swiss, etc.).
   

Day 7

 Admire the fantastic landscapes. Cable car included to Grindelwald first. Lunch included. Today, you would travel through fascinating mountains. You would pass through Interlaken and drive through the outline of its beautiful lake. Halt and take a stroll at Interlaken. Later, you would arrive at Grindelwald where you can take a break and sip some coffee. Take a Cable car ride up to Grindelwald First. Then, walk for 30 minutes along the path in which you would be able to admire among the fantastic scenery enjoying glaciers surrounding the place. You would be provided with the lunch which includes vegetarian option also. After that, continue towards Lucerne, a city which has preserved the medieval architecture. Later, return to the heart of Zurich and enjoy the evening at the city’s most popular restaurants which will provide a variety of cuisines like Indian, Oriental, Middle Eastern, Swiss, etc.
   

Day 8

 Route crosses marvellous and breathtaking landscapes. Visit to the Ice Cave. Embark on a scenic trip to Bern, capital city built around a bend in the Aare River, with its sandstone facades and medieval architecture preserved in the Old Town. You would travel through the Alps and enjoy the panoramic views that would come on the way. To explore the city, take stroll in the narrow streets of the city and breathe in the fresh air. After this, continue to Meiringen by following the beautiful lakes. Take a halt here, and enjoy your lunch. Then, travel to a mesmerizing mountain pass at an elevation of 2,429 metres known as Furka Pass. The snow-clad mountains and snake-like roads gives a panoramic view which a will be embedded in your forever. Halt at the Ice Cave for some fun and explore the viewpoint over the Rhone Glacier. (Note: Until early June and after the beginning of October, the mountain pass may be closed due to snow. In this case the alternative visit will be to the Areschlucht gorges in Meiringen.) Then, you would travel to Milan and reach there by the end of the afternoon. The place is known for its high-end restaurants, cafes, fashion and stock-exchange. You would have enough time to dine at the Porta Venecia (Port of Venice) where you can find excellent variety of restaurants offering international cuisine. After experiencing an exquisite dine-out, head back to your hotel for a comfortable night stay.
   

Day 9

 Centre of Milano | Leaning Tower of Pisa. Spend some time in Milan and explore the metropolis. You would get a chance to see its great cathedral and the galleries in the centre. Milan is popularly known for its 20th century art, architecture and it is a hi-tech hub to Google, Apple and Microsoft. Embark on a journey towards Pisa, in central Italy. Pisa is known for its Gothic churches, Romanesque architecture and lively street life. Enjoy a ride on a little tourist train and travel to the Piazza Dei Miracoli (Square of Miracles). Popular as the most beautiful urban space, here you can admire the most complex and alluring art in Italy, including the well-known Leaning Tower of Pisa. Then, continue towards Rome. You would arrive at the end of the afternoon. Relax and unwind at the hotel.
   

Day 10

 City tour of Rome. Roman Coliseum (admission price included) and visit to the Vatican City. In the morning, head out for a scenic tour of the city, for an introduction to ‘The Eternal city’. First, you would visit the tiny state of the Vatican, surrounded by Rome. The Vatican City is the headquarters of Roman Catholic Church. The place is famous for Roman sculptures, art and architecture. You would visit the St. Peter’s Basilica, an Italian Church. And you would also get a chance to go to the Roman Coliseum, the principal symbol of Rome. Built of sand and concrete, the largest oval amphitheatre is erected in the centre of Rome. This marvellous piece, constructed 2,000 years ago, will transport you to the ancient society in the Roman Empire. In the evening, transport would be provided to the Via Cavour area, a street named after Camillo Cavour. The place provides restaurants that serve lip-smacking international dishes. Later, retire to the hotel for a comfortable overnight stay.
   

Day 11

 Tour with a local expert from Florence. View from Piazzale Michelangelo. In the morning, you would head north from Rome. Before reaching the destination, admire the beautiful landscapes you would see on your route. Florence is a hub of masterpieces of Renaissance art and architecture. Upon your arrival in Florence, you would head straight to Piazzale Michelangelo, an impressive site in the city. In the evening, you would be provided a tour with a local expert, and take a stroll through the historic centre as the city is meant for walking. You would walk over the bridge, Ponte Vecchio, over the Arno River and then, explore the Duomo. It is the main Italian cathedral church and was begun in 1296 in Gothic style. The Piazza della Signoria is an L-shaped main square in Florence which offers a panoramic view of this Renaissance city. This city is truly an outdoor museum. You have rest of the day at leisure. In the evening, you can dine at one of the restaurants serving Italian, Indian, Chinese and international food. Later, return to the hotel for a cozy sleep.
   

Day 12

 Vaporetto boat on Venice’s Grand Canal. Visit and admission to the San Marcos Basilica, where we can admire the technique of crafting Murano Glass. Today, you would enjoy the marvellous mountain landscapes while travelling though the Apennine Mountains. After you arrive in Venice, board a Vapporetto, a waterbus, to travel around on the canals in the historic centre. Then, you would visit Plaza de San Marcos and enter the marvellous basilica (entry cost included). Afterwards, you would get a chance to visit the Murano glass factory city. You would see how fancy items are made out of glasses through crystal blowing. Some old techniques are still used to make the glass figurines and chandeliers. You have rest of the day at leisure, so you can explore the city at your own pace. At the end of the afternoon, you would get a chance to dine-in the Mestre area that offers a variety of cuisines including Indian, Oriental, Italian, etc. Later, retire to bed for a comfortable overnight stay.
   

Day 13

 Verona, the city of Romeo y Juliet. Breath-taking landscapes. Today, in the morning, you would depart from Venice and travel to Verona. The guide will instruct you to the places in this Romeo and Juliet’s city - the city of lovers! The place still has one of the world’s great opera venues. Afterwards, continue the journey between mountains to Austria. It is known for its Imperial history, baroque architecture and mountains. Upon your arrival in Salzburg, the guide would accompany you to the most beautiful streets of the historic centre of this city which is declared as a World Heritage site. The city is situated on the border of Germany. It looks mesmerizing with overlooking Eastern Alps and Salzach River flowing in the city. Explore the city on your own pace. Note: In case of few travellers on the trip, the journey from Verona to Salzburg may be done partially by a train.
   

Day 14

 Vienna city tour. Gardens of the immense Schön¬brunn Palace. Bid an adieu to Salzburg and travel to Vienna at noon. Once you reach the city, start with a scenic visit around this impressive city with a local expert. The city is known for its grand palaces, vibrant city centre and majestic avenues. Then, directly head towards the Schönbrunn Palace, a summer residence known for its culture and architecture. Take a stroll around and enjoy its beautiful gardens. In the evening, you would go to the City Hall Square, a public square. Here, you can shop and enjoy the lighting and the nightlife in the neighbouring streets. You can dine at your preferred restaurant with cuisines from around the world including Austrian, Chinese, Indian and Italian. Later, retire to the hotel for a comfortable night stay.
   

Day 15

 Early in the morning, have a sumptuous breakfast. Today, the European tour and services end.
